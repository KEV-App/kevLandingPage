FAQ

Color temperature, white balance & KEV.

Short answers to the questions photographers, videographers and gaffers ask the most.

What is a white balance meter?

A white balance meter measures the color of light in a scene and tells you the color temperature in Kelvin (and tint), so you can set your camera's white balance to match. KEV does this in real time using your iPhone or iPad camera. Read the full guide →

What does color temperature in Kelvin mean?

Color temperature is how warm or cool a light source looks, measured in Kelvin (K). Tungsten is roughly 2800-3200K (warm), daylight is around 5500K, and shade or overcast sky is 6000K+ (cool). Setting your camera's white balance to the Kelvin value of the light makes whites look white. Kelvin scale, explained →

How do I measure color temperature with my iPhone?

Install KEV, open the app and point your iPhone or iPad camera at the scene or light source. KEV uses AI to analyze the live preview and shows the color temperature in Kelvin (plus tint) in real time. No external sensor needed. Step-by-step guide →

Is KEV an accurate light meter?

KEV was calibrated against a Sekonic C-700R-U SpectroMaster Color Meter and is rated 4.5★ by 1,000+ users on the App Store. Accuracy ranges from fair to near 100% depending on lighting conditions and your device's camera.

Can I use KEV as a tint meter?

Yes. KEV reads tint (magenta/green shift) alongside Kelvin, so you can correct green LED or magenta cast in-camera instead of fixing it in post. Tint vs Kelvin →

Does KEV work for video and filmmaking?

Yes. KEV is used by videographers, cinematographers and gaffers to balance ambient and key light, match white balance across cameras, gel HMI to tungsten and dial in practical LEDs — saving time in post-production.

What is the best white balance for daylight?

For midday sun on a clear day, around 5500K is the standard "daylight" reference. For shade or overcast skies, use 6500-7500K. KEV gives you the exact Kelvin number for the scene you're actually in.
